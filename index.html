01 — Films / Director

Soil Story

Director202516mmExperimental Documentary

Soil Story is a 16mm film, combining found foliage and an experimental approach to the film developing process that is in collaboration with the environment. The film explores the juxtaposing histories and memories of the Ridgewood Reservoir and the Cemetery Belt. Two locations that straddle each other with the Jackie Robinson Parkway cut in between.

With both locations in conversation with each other, we explore the cycles of time, space, memory and land development and disposal.

Screenings
  • Science New Wave Film Festival (2025)
  • Soil and Sound Waves at the Rockaways (2025)
  • Urban Soils: Soil Symposium (2025)
02 — Films / Director

Seeing Things, Dreaming Things

Director / Co-creator202116mm + Hi-8 TapePersonal Documentary

Seeing Things, Dreaming Things is a personal documentary that explores memories and dreams, in an effort to organize these subjective and personal experiences into something more logical and concrete.

Filmed on 16mm film and Hi-8 Tape. Created by Olivia Acuña, Editing by Phillip Thompson.

03 — Films / Cinematographer · Producer · Colorist

Altar Piece

Cinematographer / Producer / Colorist2025Documentary

The Holy Mary amidst ongoing traffic. A basement of deities with the light on 24/7. A collection of trash, or so it seems.

“Altar Piece” explores Ridgewood, Queens, through the altars and shrines that ornament the neighborhood. While some are more recognizable in form than others, these objects are brought together with a considered touch.

05 — Films / Cinematographer

In Love, In Time

Cinematographer201916mmShort Film

In Love, In Time, follows a narrative of a woman who is in two different relationships during two periods of her life, but the emotions that the relationships produce are the same. The film is meant to explore the idea of relationship dependency amongst youth, and one’s identity solely being tied to another.

Directed by Philip Thompson. Cinematography by Olivia Acuna. 16mm.

About / Contact

Olivia Acuña

Olivia Acuña is an interdisciplinary artist and filmmaker based in Brooklyn. Olivia is obsessed with the materiality of film and explores themes of memory, magical realism and the human condition within her work. Often working with 16mm film, analog equipment and experimenting with different processing techniques.

In 2025 Olivia took part in an 8 month artist residency at Union Docs, during this residency Olivia worked on two short documentaries Soil Story and Altar Piece. Soil Story premiered at the Science New Wave Film festival and as an installation piece at The Rockaway Soils festival and Urban Soils: Soil Symposium.

Currently, Olivia works as projectionist at Anthology Film Archives and as a Colorist at Metropolis Post. She also works freelance as a DP and colorist. She is also a programmer and Co-founder of Peace Centers micro cinema in BedStuy that showcases local and independent film monthly.
